DocFill - Privacy Policy

How DocFill handles your data

Overview

DocFill is a Microsoft Word add-in that helps you fill placeholder fields in document templates. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data DocFill accesses and how it is handled.

Data Processing

DocFill processes your document content entirely within your browser. Specifically:

  • DocFill scans your Word document for {{placeholder}} markers to build a fill form.
  • When you click "Fill Document," DocFill replaces placeholders with the values you entered.
  • Filled values are tracked using Word's built-in content controls so they can be reset back to placeholders if needed.

Data Storage

DocFill stores your field configuration (labels, field types, date format preferences) in your browser's localStorage. This data:

  • Never leaves your device
  • Is not sent to any server
  • Is not shared with any third party
  • Can be cleared at any time by clearing your browser data

Data Collection

DocFill does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information. We do not use analytics, tracking, cookies, or telemetry of any kind.

Document Content Stays Local

Your document content is never transmitted to our servers. The add-in code is loaded from our hosting provider (Cloudflare Pages), and the Office.js library is loaded from Microsoft's CDN, but all document processing happens entirely within your browser. No document text, placeholder values, or filled content is ever sent anywhere.

Third Parties

DocFill does not share any data with third parties. The only external resource loaded is the Microsoft Office.js library (from Microsoft's CDN, required for all Office add-ins).

Changes to This Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update this page. If we introduce features that change how data is handled (such as server-side processing), we will update this policy before those features are released.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please reach out via our support page.
